Japanese Learning Resources

To supplement your 20-day Japanese curriculum, here are additional resources to help you learn and practice Japanese for your travels.

Pronunciation Tips

Vowels

  • あ (a) - like "a" in "father"
  • い (i) - like "ee" in "feet"
  • う (u) - like "oo" in "boot" but shorter
  • え (e) - like "e" in "pet"
  • お (o) - like "o" in "go"

Important Notes

  • Japanese is a syllable-timed language (each syllable takes roughly the same amount of time)
  • Pronounce each syllable clearly
  • The "r" sound is between an English "r" and "l"
  • Stress is generally even across syllables
  • Long vowels (marked with a line above the letter in romanization) should be held twice as long

Politeness Levels

Most phrases in this guide use the polite form (です/ます), which is appropriate for travelers in most situations. When in doubt, being more polite is better than being too casual.

Study Tips

  1. Use flashcards or a language app to review vocabulary daily
  2. Listen to Japanese audio to improve pronunciation
  3. Practice speaking out loud, even when alone
  4. Label items in your home with Japanese words
  5. Try to learn 5-10 new words each day beyond the curriculum
  6. Watch Japanese travel videos with subtitles
  7. Record yourself speaking to track improvement
  8. Find a language exchange partner online for practice

Cultural Tips for Japan Travel

  • Bowing: A slight bow is appropriate for most situations as a tourist.
  • Shoes: Remove shoes when entering homes, traditional ryokans, and some restaurants.
  • Tipping: Not customary in Japan and may cause confusion.
  • Chopsticks: Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice or pass food directly from chopsticks to chopsticks.
  • Public Behavior: Speaking loudly in public places, especially on trains, is considered impolite.
  • Masks: Wearing masks when sick is common courtesy in Japan.
  • Trash: Public trash cans are limited; be prepared to carry your trash until you find one.
  • Onsen Etiquette: Wash thoroughly before entering hot springs, and don't wear swimwear in traditional onsen.
