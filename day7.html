Basic Conversation Practice (10 minutes)

Now let's practice putting everything together in basic conversations. These will help you use what you've learned in real-life travel situations.

Conversation 1: Basic Greetings and Self-Introduction

Person A: こんにちは。 Konnichiwa. Hello.
Person B: こんにちは。お名前は？ Konnichiwa. Onamae wa? Hello. What is your name?
Person A: 私の名前は[your name]です。あなたは？ Watashi no namae wa [your name] desu. Anata wa? My name is [your name]. And you?
Person B: 私は田中です。はじめまして。 Watashi wa Tanaka desu. Hajimemashite. I am Tanaka. Nice to meet you.
Person A: はじめまして。どうぞよろしく。 Hajimemashite. Douzo yoroshiku. Nice to meet you. Please treat me well.

Conversation 2: At a Restaurant

Customer: すみません。 Sumimasen. Excuse me.
Staff: いらっしゃいませ。 Irasshaimase. Welcome.
Customer: これをください。 Kore o kudasai. This, please.
Staff: はい、ラーメンですね。お飲み物は？ Hai, ramen desu ne. Onomimono wa? Yes, ramen, right? What would you like to drink?
Customer: お水をください。 Omizu o kudasai. Water, please.
Staff: かしこまりました。 Kashikomarimashita. Understood./I'll take care of it.

Conversation 3: Asking for Directions

Tourist: すみません。駅はどこですか？ Sumimasen. Eki wa doko desu ka? Excuse me. Where is the station?
Local: 駅ですか？あそこです。 Eki desu ka? Asoko desu. The station? It's over there.
Tourist: ありがとうございます。 Arigatou gozaimasu. Thank you very much.
Local: どういたしまして。 Dou itashimashite. You're welcome.

Practice Tips

  • Try reading each conversation aloud, focusing on proper pronunciation
  • Practice with a friend or language exchange partner if possible
  • Record yourself saying the Japanese phrases to review your pronunciation
  • Memorize one conversation at a time
  • Try creating your own variations using vocabulary you've learned

Bonus Activity: Start Learning Katakana

If you're feeling confident with hiragana and have extra time, you can get a head start on katakana, which we'll begin learning next week. Here are the first few katakana characters:

First Five Katakana Characters

Katakana Hiragana Equivalent Romaji Mnemonic
ア あ a Looks like an "A" with a stick in the middle
イ い i Looks like the letter "I" with two short strokes
ウ う u Resembles a square smile with a short stroke on top
エ え e Similar to the letter "E" with the middle line extended
オ お o Like an "O" with a short stick on the side

Katakana is primarily used for foreign words, names, and loan words. We'll cover this in more detail starting on Day 8.
