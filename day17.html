Review Day 16: Sightseeing & Tourist Attractions

Yesterday, we learned vocabulary and phrases related to tourist attractions and sightseeing. Let's review some key phrases:

  • 〜はどこですか？ (... wa doko desu ka?) - Where is ...?
  • おすすめの場所は何ですか？ (Osusume no basho wa nan desu ka?) - What places do you recommend?
  • 写真を撮ってもいいですか？ (Shashin o totte mo ii desu ka?) - May I take a photo?

Izakaya Culture

Izakayas are Japanese-style pubs where people go to socialize over drinks and small dishes. They're a great place to experience Japanese food culture in a relaxed atmosphere.

Cultural Note:

At an izakaya, it's common to order several small dishes that everyone shares, rather than individual large meals. This is called "モッタイナイ" (mottainai) culture, where sharing food is considered more economical and enjoyable.

Useful Izakaya Phrases

乾杯！ (かんぱい！)

Kanpai!

Cheers!

Said when raising glasses before drinking

お勧めは何ですか？ (おすすめはなんですか？)

Osusume wa nan desu ka?

What do you recommend?

メニューを見せてください。 (メニューをみせてください。)

Menyuu o misete kudasai.

Please show me the menu.

これをください。 (これをください。)

Kore o kudasai.

I'll have this, please.

Use while pointing to an item on the menu

お会計お願いします。 (おかいけいおねがいします。)

Okaikei onegaishimasu.

Check, please.

別々で払います。 (べつべつではらいます。)

Betsubetsu de haraimasu.

We'll pay separately.

Practice: Ordering at an Izakaya

Example Dialogue:

Staff: いらっしゃいませ！何名様ですか？ (Irasshaimase! Nan mei-sama desu ka?)
Welcome! How many people?

You: 二人です。 (Futari desu.)
Two people.

Staff: こちらへどうぞ。 (Kochira e douzo.)
This way, please.

Staff: ご注文は何にしますか？ (Go-chuumon wa nani ni shimasu ka?)
What would you like to order?

You: 生ビール二つと枝豆をください。 (Nama biiru futatsu to edamame o kudasai.)
Two draft beers and edamame, please.

You: あと、お勧めは何ですか？ (Ato, osusume wa nan desu ka?)
Also, what do you recommend?

Staff: 今日は焼き鳥が新鮮ですよ。 (Kyou wa yakitori ga shinsen desu yo.)
Today, the yakitori is fresh.

You: じゃあ、焼き鳥もお願いします。 (Jaa, yakitori mo onegaishimasu.)
Then, yakitori as well, please.

Homework
