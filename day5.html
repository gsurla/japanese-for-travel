Review Day 4 (5 minutes)

Let's quickly review the essential vocabulary we learned yesterday:

Places:

空港 (kūkō) - airport, 駅 (eki) - station, ホテル (hoteru) - hotel, レストラン (resutoran) - restaurant, トイレ (toire) - toilet, コンビニ (konbini) - convenience store, 銀行 (ginkō) - bank

Transportation:

電車 (densha) - train, バス (basu) - bus, タクシー (takushī) - taxi, 地下鉄 (chikatetsu) - subway, 切符 (kippu) - ticket

Essential Items:

お金 (o-kane) - money, パスポート (pasupōto) - passport, カード (kādo) - card, 鍵 (kagi) - key, 携帯電話 (keitai denwa) - mobile phone, Wi-Fi (wai-fai) - Wi-Fi

Basic Sentence Structure (10 minutes)

Japanese sentence structure follows a Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) pattern, which is different from the English Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) pattern. Let's learn the basic structure.

Basic Pattern: [Topic] は [Comment] です

This is the fundamental pattern for simple statements in Japanese:

  • Topic: What you're talking about
  • は (wa): Topic marker particle
  • Comment: What you're saying about the topic
  • です (desu): The "to be" verb (is/am/are)

これはホテルです。

Kore wa hoteru desu.

This is a hotel.

これ (this) + は (topic marker) + ホテル (hotel) + です (is)

わたしは学生です。

Watashi wa gakusei desu.

I am a student.

わたし (I) + は (topic marker) + 学生 (student) + です (am)

Negative Sentences: [Topic] は [Comment] ではありません

To make a negative sentence, we replace です with ではありません:

これはホテルではありません。

Kore wa hoteru dewa arimasen.

This is not a hotel.

これ (this) + は (topic marker) + ホテル (hotel) + ではありません (is not)

Existence Sentences: [Thing] が あります/います

To express that something exists, we use あります (for inanimate objects) or います (for living things):

ここにトイレがあります。

Koko ni toire ga arimasu.

There is a toilet here.

ここに (here) + トイレ (toilet) + が (subject marker) + あります (exists)

Polite Expressions (5 minutes)

These polite expressions will be very useful during your travels in Japan.

すみません (Sumimasen)

Pronunciation: soo-mee-mah-sen

Meaning: Excuse me / I'm sorry

Usage: Multi-purpose expression used to get attention, apologize for minor inconveniences, or express gratitude.

Cultural note: One of the most versatile and frequently used expressions in Japanese.

お願いします (Onegaishimasu)

Pronunciation: oh-neh-gah-ee-shee-mahs

Meaning: Please

Usage: Used when making requests or asking for something.

Cultural note: Often accompanied by a slight bow when making requests.

失礼します (Shitsurei shimasu)

Pronunciation: shee-tsoo-ray shee-mahs

Meaning: Excuse me (when interrupting or leaving)

Usage: Used when entering a room, leaving early, or interrupting someone.

Cultural note: Literally means "I'm being rude" but is a polite expression.
