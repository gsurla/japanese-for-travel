Asking for Directions and Help While Sightseeing

Getting around in Japan can be challenging, especially in areas with limited English signage. These phrases will help you ask for directions, understand responses, and get assistance while sightseeing.

Basic Direction Questions

Japanese Pronunciation English
＿＿はどこですか？ __ wa doko desu ka? Where is __?
＿＿への行き方を教えてください。 __ e no ikikata wo oshiete kudasai. Please tell me how to get to __.
駅はどこですか？ Eki wa doko desu ka? Where is the station?
トイレはどこですか？ Toire wa doko desu ka? Where is the bathroom?
近くに＿＿はありますか？ Chikaku ni __ wa arimasu ka? Is there a __ nearby?

Distance and Time

Japanese Pronunciation English
どのくらい歩きますか？ Dono kurai arukimasu ka? How far is it to walk?
＿＿分くらいです。 __ fun kurai desu. About __ minutes.
近いです。 Chikai desu. It's close.
遠いです。 Tooi desu. It's far.
タクシーに乗った方がいいです。 Takushii ni notta hou ga ii desu. It's better to take a taxi.

Sightseeing Questions

Japanese Pronunciation English
開館時間は何時ですか？ Kaikan jikan wa nanji desu ka? What time does it open?
閉館時間は何時ですか？ Heikan jikan wa nanji desu ka? What time does it close?
入場料はいくらですか？ Nyuujouryou wa ikura desu ka? How much is the admission fee?

Asking for Help

Japanese Pronunciation English
すみません、助けていただけますか？ Sumimasen, tasukete itadakemasu ka? Excuse me, could you help me?
道に迷いました。 Michi ni mayoimashita. I'm lost.
英語を話せる人はいますか？ Eigo wo hanaseru hito wa imasu ka? Is there someone who speaks English?
これは何ですか？ Kore wa nan desu ka? What is this?
写真を撮っていただけますか？ Shashin wo totte itadakemasu ka? Could you take a photo for me?

Sightseeing Tips in Japan

  • Tourist Information Centers: Look for tourist information centers (観光案内所 - kankou annai-jo) in major stations and tourist areas. Staff often speak English and can provide maps and guidance.
  • Google Maps: Works well in Japan and can show train/bus routes. Download offline maps before your trip.
  • Pointing at Maps: Carrying a physical map can be helpful. If you can't communicate verbally, pointing at your destination on a map is widely understood.
  • Police Boxes: Police boxes (交番 - kouban) are found throughout Japan. Officers can help with directions and lost items.
  • Train Station Staff: Station attendants can help with transportation questions and often speak some English.
  • Writing Down Addresses: Have your hotel's address written in Japanese to show taxi drivers or locals when asking for directions.
