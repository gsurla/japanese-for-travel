Review Day 14: Weather & Seasons Vocabulary

Let's quickly review the weather vocabulary and phrases we learned yesterday:

  • 「天気」(tenki) - weather
  • 「晴れ」(hare) - sunny
  • 「雨」(ame) - rain
  • 「曇り」(kumori) - cloudy
  • 「雪」(yuki) - snow
  • 「暑い」(atsui) - hot
  • 「寒い」(samui) - cold
  • 「今日の天気はどうですか？」(Kyou no tenki wa dou desu ka?) - How is the weather today?
  • 「雨が降っています」(Ame ga futte imasu) - It's raining

Small Talk Vocabulary

Making small talk is an important social skill in any culture. In Japan, casual conversation helps build relationships and shows politeness. Let's learn key vocabulary for making small talk:

はじめまして (hajimemashite) - Nice to meet you (first time)

Pronunciation: ha-ji-me-ma-shi-te

Example: はじめまして、ジョンです。 (Hajimemashite, Jon desu.) - Nice to meet you, I'm John.

Cultural note: Usually accompanied by a bow when meeting someone for the first time

お元気ですか？ (ogenki desu ka?) - How are you?

Pronunciation: o-gen-ki des-ka

Example: お元気ですか？ (Ogenki desu ka?) - How are you?

Cultural note: This is not asked as frequently as "How are you?" in English, but is still common among friends

元気です (genki desu) - I'm fine/well

Pronunciation: gen-ki des

Example: はい、元気です。 (Hai, genki desu.) - Yes, I'm fine.

Cultural note: A common and positive response to "Ogenki desu ka?"

お仕事は何ですか？ (oshigoto wa nan desu ka?) - What is your job/occupation?

Pronunciation: o-shi-go-to wa nan des-ka

Example: お仕事は何ですか？ (Oshigoto wa nan desu ka?) - What is your job?

Cultural note: Occupation is a common small talk topic in Japan

趣味は何ですか？ (shumi wa nan desu ka?) - What are your hobbies?

Pronunciation: shu-mi wa nan des-ka

Example: 趣味は何ですか？ (Shumi wa nan desu ka?) - What are your hobbies?

Cultural note: Discussing hobbies is a safe and common small talk topic

Common Small Talk Topics:

  • 出身 (shusshin) - hometown/origin
  • 家族 (kazoku) - family
  • 旅行 (ryokou) - travel
  • 食べ物 (tabemono) - food
  • スポーツ (supootsu) - sports
  • 映画 (eiga) - movies
  • 音楽 (ongaku) - music
  • 本 (hon) - books
  • テレビ (terebi) - television
  • 週末 (shuumatsu) - weekend

Useful Adjectives for Opinions:

  • おいしい (oishii) - delicious
  • 楽しい (tanoshii) - fun/enjoyable
  • 面白い (omoshiroi) - interesting/funny
  • 素晴らしい (subarashii) - wonderful/excellent
  • きれい (kirei) - beautiful/clean
  • 難しい (muzukashii) - difficult
  • 簡単 (kantan) - easy/simple
  • 忙しい (isogashii) - busy
  • 疲れた (tsukareta) - tired
  • 嬉しい (ureshii) - happy/glad

Conversation Fillers and Responses:

  • そうですね (sou desu ne) - That's right/I agree
  • なるほど (naruhodo) - I see/I understand
  • 本当ですか？ (hontou desu ka?) - Really?
  • すごいですね (sugoi desu ne) - That's amazing
  • そうですか (sou desu ka) - Is that so?
  • えーと (eeto) - Um/Let me see
  • あのう (anou) - Um/Excuse me
  • じゃあ (jaa) - Well then

Small Talk Phrases

Let's learn essential phrases for making small talk in Japanese:

日本は初めてですか？
Nihon wa hajimete desu ka?
Is this your first time in Japan?
A common question asked to foreign visitors
  • 日本 (Nihon) - Japan
  • は (wa) - topic marker
  • 初めて (hajimete) - first time
  • ですか (desu ka) - is it?

A: 日本は初めてですか？ (Nihon wa hajimete desu ka?)
Is this your first time in Japan?

B: いいえ、2回目です。 (Iie, nikai-me desu.)
No, it's my second time.

A: どこに行きましたか？ (Doko ni ikimashita ka?)
Where did you go?

B: 東京と京都に行きました。 (Tokyo to Kyoto ni ikimashita.)
I went to Tokyo and Kyoto.

Variations:

  • ＿＿は初めてですか？ (__ wa hajimete desu ka?) - Is this your first time in/at __?
  • 日本に何回来ましたか？ (Nihon ni nankai kimashita ka?) - How many times have you been to Japan?
日本の印象はどうですか？
Nihon no inshou wa dou desu ka?
What is your impression of Japan?
A good follow-up question after asking if it's their first time in Japan
  • 日本 (Nihon) - Japan
  • の (no) - possessive particle
  • 印象 (inshou) - impression
  • は (wa) - topic marker
  • どう (dou) - how
  • ですか (desu ka) - is it?

A: 日本の印象はどうですか？ (Nihon no inshou wa dou desu ka?)
What is your impression of Japan?

B: とてもきれいで、人々が親切です。 (Totemo kirei de, hitobito ga shinsetsu desu.)
It's very clean, and the people are kind.

A: ありがとうございます。 (Arigatou gozaimasu.)
Thank you.

Variations:

  • 東京の印象はどうですか？ (Tokyo no inshou wa dou desu ka?) - What is your impression of Tokyo?
  • 日本食の印象はどうですか？ (Nihonshoku no inshou wa dou desu ka?) - What is your impression of Japanese food?
出身はどちらですか？
Shusshin wa dochira desu ka?
Where are you from?
A polite way to ask someone's place of origin
  • 出身 (shusshin) - origin/hometown
  • は (wa) - topic marker
  • どちら (dochira) - where (polite form)
  • ですか (desu ka) - is it?

A: 出身はどちらですか？ (Shusshin wa dochira desu ka?)
Where are you from?

B: アメリカ出身です。 (Amerika shusshin desu.)
I'm from America.

A: どの州ですか？ (Dono shuu desu ka?)
Which state?

B: カリフォルニア州です。 (Kariforunia-shuu desu.)
California.

Variations:

  • どこから来ましたか？ (Doko kara kimashita ka?) - Where did you come from?
  • お国はどちらですか？ (Okuni wa dochira desu ka?) - Which country are you from? (very polite)
趣味は何ですか？
Shumi wa nan desu ka?
What are your hobbies?
A common small talk question to get to know someone better
  • 趣味 (shumi) - hobby
  • は (wa) - topic marker
  • 何 (nan) - what
  • ですか (desu ka) - is it?

A: 趣味は何ですか？ (Shumi wa nan desu ka?)
What are your hobbies?

B: 写真を撮ることです。 (Shashin wo toru koto desu.)
Taking photographs.

A: 素晴らしいですね。何の写真を撮りますか？ (Subarashii desu ne. Nan no shashin wo torimasu ka?)
That's wonderful. What kind of photos do you take?

B: 風景の写真が好きです。 (Fuukei no shashin ga suki desu.)
I like landscape photos.

Variations:

  • 休みの日は何をしますか？ (Yasumi no hi wa nani wo shimasu ka?) - What do you do on your days off?
  • 好きな活動は何ですか？ (Sukina katsudou wa nan desu ka?) - What activities do you like?
日本料理は好きですか？
Nihon ryouri wa suki desu ka?
Do you like Japanese food?
Food is a popular small talk topic in Japan
  • 日本料理 (Nihon ryouri) - Japanese cuisine
  • は (wa) - topic marker
  • 好き (suki) - like
  • ですか (desu ka) - is it?

A: 日本料理は好きですか？ (Nihon ryouri wa suki desu ka?)
Do you like Japanese food?

B: はい、大好きです。特に寿司が好きです。 (Hai, daisuki desu. Tokuni sushi ga suki desu.)
Yes, I love it. I especially like sushi.

A: 他に好きな日本食は何ですか？ (Hoka ni sukina Nihonshoku wa nan desu ka?)
What other Japanese foods do you like?

B: ラーメンも好きです。 (Raamen mo suki desu.)
I also like ramen.

Variations:

  • 何の食べ物が好きですか？ (Nan no tabemono ga suki desu ka?) - What kind of food do you like?
  • 好きな日本料理は何ですか？ (Sukina Nihon ryouri wa nan desu ka?) - What Japanese food do you like?

Additional Useful Small Talk Phrases:

  • 日本語が上手ですね。 (Nihongo ga jouzu desu ne.)
    Your Japanese is good.
  • どのくらい日本にいますか？ (Dono kurai Nihon ni imasu ka?)
    How long will you be in Japan?
  • 日本で何をしたいですか？ (Nihon de nani wo shitai desu ka?)
    What do you want to do in Japan?
  • お仕事は何ですか？ (Oshigoto wa nan desu ka?)
    What is your job?
  • 家族で来ましたか？ (Kazoku de kimashita ka?)
    Did you come with your family?
  • また会いましょう。 (Mata aimashou.)
    Let's meet again.

Casual Conversation Practice

Let's practice putting together small talk phrases in longer conversations:

Scenario 1: Meeting Someone New

A: こんにちは。はじめまして、マイクです。
(Konnichiwa. Hajimemashite, Maiku desu.)
Hello. Nice to meet you, I'm Mike.

B: はじめまして、ナオコです。
(Hajimemashite, Naoko desu.)
Nice to meet you, I'm Naoko.

A: 出身はどちらですか？
(Shusshin wa dochira desu ka?)
Where are you from?

B: 大阪出身です。マイクさんは？
(Osaka shusshin desu. Maiku-san wa?)
I'm from Osaka. And you, Mike?

A: カナダ出身です。日本は初めてです。
(Kanada shusshin desu. Nihon wa hajimete desu.)
I'm from Canada. This is my first time in Japan.

B: 日本の印象はどうですか？
(Nihon no inshou wa dou desu ka?)
What is your impression of Japan?

A: とても素晴らしいです。みんな親切ですね。
(Totemo subarashii desu. Minna shinsetsu desu ne.)
It's very wonderful. Everyone is kind, aren't they?

Scenario 2: Discussing Hobbies

A: 趣味は何ですか？
(Shumi wa nan desu ka?)
What are your hobbies?

B: 音楽を聴くことと料理をすることです。あなたは？
(Ongaku wo kiku koto to ryouri wo suru koto desu. Anata wa?)
Listening to music and cooking. And you?

A: 私はスポーツが好きです。特にサッカーをするのが好きです。
(Watashi wa supootsu ga suki desu. Tokuni sakkaa wo suru no ga suki desu.)
I like sports. I especially like playing soccer.

B: そうですか。日本のサッカーチームは知っていますか？
(Sou desu ka. Nihon no sakkaa chiimu wa shitte imasu ka?)
Is that so? Do you know any Japanese soccer teams?

A: はい、川崎フロンターレが好きです。
(Hai, Kawasaki Furontaare ga suki desu.)
Yes, I like Kawasaki Frontale.

Scenario 3: Talking About Food

A: 日本料理は好きですか？
(Nihon ryouri wa suki desu ka?)
Do you like Japanese food?

B: はい、大好きです。特に寿司が好きです。
(Hai, daisuki desu. Tokuni sushi ga suki desu.)
Yes, I love it. I especially like sushi.

A: 私もです。東京で美味しい寿司屋を知っていますか？
(Watashi mo desu. Tokyo de oishii sushiya wo shitte imasu ka?)
Me too. Do you know any delicious sushi restaurants in Tokyo?

B: はい、築地市場の近くに良い店があります。今度ご案内しましょうか？
(Hai, Tsukiji shijou no chikaku ni yoi mise ga arimasu. Kondo go-annai shimashou ka?)
Yes, there's a good restaurant near Tsukiji market. Shall I take you there sometime?

A: はい、ぜひお願いします。楽しみにしています。
(Hai, zehi onegaishimasu. Tanoshimi ni shite imasu.)
Yes, please do. I'm looking forward to it.

Homework

  1. Create flashcards with the small talk phrases learned today and practice them daily.
  2. Write out a complete self-introduction in Japanese that includes your name, country of origin, occupation/student status, and hobbies.
  3. Practice the three conversation scenarios with a study partner or by yourself, taking both roles.
  4. Come up with five personal responses to common small talk questions (about your hobbies, food preferences, etc.).
  5. Research one Japanese cultural aspect you're interested in and prepare a few sentences to discuss it in Japanese.
  6. For extra practice: Record yourself saying the small talk phrases to improve your pronunciation.
