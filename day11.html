Shopping Vocabulary

Shopping in Japan is an enjoyable experience! Let's learn essential vocabulary for shopping at various stores:

Basic Shopping Vocabulary:

お店 (omise) - store/shop

Pronunciation: o-mi-se

Example: このお店は何時に開きますか？ (Kono omise wa nanji ni akimasu ka?) - What time does this store open?

Cultural note: Most department stores in Japan open around 10:00 AM and close at 8:00 PM

値段 (nedan) - price

Pronunciation: ne-dan

Example: この値段は高いです (Kono nedan wa takai desu) - This price is high

Cultural note: Haggling is not common in most Japanese stores, but sometimes possible at markets

レシート (reshiito) - receipt

Pronunciation: re-shii-to

Example: レシートをください (Reshiito wo kudasai) - Please give me a receipt

Cultural note: If you're a tourist, keep your receipts for potential tax refunds

税込み (zeikomi) - tax included

Pronunciation: zei-ko-mi

Example: この価格は税込みですか？ (Kono kakaku wa zeikomi desu ka?) - Is this price tax included?

Cultural note: Most displayed prices in Japan include tax (currently 10% in most cases)

クレジットカード (kurejitto kaado) - credit card

Pronunciation: ku-re-jit-to kaa-do

Example: クレジットカードは使えますか？ (Kurejitto kaado wa tsukaemasu ka?) - Can I use a credit card?

Cultural note: While cash is still preferred in many small shops, credit cards are accepted in most tourist areas

現金 (genkin) - cash

Pronunciation: gen-kin

Example: 現金しか使えません (Genkin shika tsukaemasen) - Only cash is accepted

Cultural note: Japan is still largely a cash-based society, so carrying cash is recommended

Types of Stores:

  • デパート (depaato) - department store
  • スーパー (suupaa) - supermarket
  • コンビニ (konbini) - convenience store
  • ドラッグストア (doraggu sutoa) - drugstore
  • 電気店 (denkiten) - electronics store
  • 本屋 (honya) - bookstore
  • 100円ショップ (hyaku-en shoppu) - 100 yen shop
  • 市場 (ichiba) - market
  • 商店街 (shoutengai) - shopping street

Shopping Items:

  • 服 (fuku) - clothes
  • 靴 (kutsu) - shoes
  • カバン (kaban) - bag
  • お土産 (omiyage) - souvenir
  • ギフト (gifuto) - gift
  • アクセサリー (akusesarii) - accessory
  • 電子機器 (denshi kiki) - electronic devices
  • 日用品 (nichiyouhin) - daily necessities

Shopping Related Words:

  • 買う (kau) - to buy
  • 売る (uru) - to sell
  • 試着する (shichaku suru) - to try on (clothes)
  • サイズ (saizu) - size
  • 色 (iro) - color
  • ブランド (burando) - brand
  • 割引 (waribiki) - discount
  • セール (seeru) - sale
  • 袋 (fukuro) - bag (for your purchases)

Essential Shopping Phrases

Let's learn key phrases that will help you shop in Japan:

1. これはいくらですか？ (Kore wa ikura desu ka?) - How much is this?

Pronunciation: ko-re wa i-ku-ra des-ka

Usage: This is probably the most essential shopping phrase

Structure:

  • これ (kore) - this
  • は (wa) - topic marker
  • いくら (ikura) - how much
  • ですか (desu ka) - is?

Example dialogue:

A: すみません、これはいくらですか？ (Sumimasen, kore wa ikura desu ka?)
Excuse me, how much is this?

B: 2,500円です。 (Ni-sen go-hyaku en desu.)
It's 2,500 yen.

A: ありがとうございます。 (Arigatou gozaimasu.)
Thank you.

Variations:

  • それはいくらですか？ (Sore wa ikura desu ka?)
    How much is that? (for something the seller is holding)
  • あれはいくらですか？ (Are wa ikura desu ka?)
    How much is that over there?

2. ～を探しています (... wo sagashite imasu) - I'm looking for ...

Pronunciation: wo sa-ga-shi-te i-ma-su

Usage: Use this phrase when you need help finding a specific item

Structure:

  • ～ (item name)
  • を (wo) - object marker
  • 探しています (sagashite imasu) - am looking for

Example dialogue:

A: すみません、日本茶を探しています。 (Sumimasen, nihon-cha wo sagashite imasu.)
Excuse me, I'm looking for Japanese tea.

B: 日本茶は2階にあります。 (Nihon-cha wa ni-kai ni arimasu.)
Japanese tea is on the second floor.

A: ありがとうございます。 (Arigatou gozaimasu.)
Thank you.

More examples:

  • お土産を探しています。 (Omiyage wo sagashite imasu.)
    I'm looking for souvenirs.
  • Tシャツを探しています。 (T-shatsu wo sagashite imasu.)
    I'm looking for T-shirts.
  • 安い電子辞書を探しています。 (Yasui denshi jisho wo sagashite imasu.)
    I'm looking for an inexpensive electronic dictionary.

3. これをください。 (Kore wo kudasai.) - I'll take this, please.

Pronunciation: ko-re wo ku-da-sa-i

Usage: Use this phrase when you've decided to purchase an item

Structure:

  • これ (kore) - this
  • を (wo) - object marker
  • ください (kudasai) - please give me

Example dialogue:

A: これをください。 (Kore wo kudasai.)
I'll take this, please.

B: はい、3,000円です。 (Hai, san-zen en desu.)
Yes, that's 3,000 yen.

A: どうぞ。 (Douzo.)
Here you are.

Variations:

  • これを2つください。 (Kore wo futatsu kudasai.)
    I'll take two of these, please.
  • 赤いのをください。 (Akai no wo kudasai.)
    I'll take the red one, please.

4. 試着できますか？ (Shichaku dekimasu ka?) - Can I try it on?

Pronunciation: shi-cha-ku de-ki-mas-ka

Usage: Use this phrase when shopping for clothes

Structure:

  • 試着 (shichaku) - trying on clothes
  • できますか (dekimasu ka) - can I do?

Example dialogue:

A: このシャツを試着できますか？ (Kono shatsu wo shichaku dekimasu ka?)
Can I try on this shirt?

B: はい、試着室はあちらです。 (Hai, shichakushitsu wa achira desu.)
Yes, the fitting room is over there.

A: ありがとうございます。 (Arigatou gozaimasu.)
Thank you.

Additional Useful Shopping Phrases:

  • クレジットカードは使えますか？ (Kurejitto kaado wa tsukaemasu ka?)
    Can I use a credit card?
  • 免税になりますか？ (Menzei ni narimasu ka?)
    Is this tax-free?
  • もっと安いのはありますか？ (Motto yasui no wa arimasu ka?)
    Do you have anything cheaper?
  • 別の色はありますか？ (Betsu no iro wa arimasu ka?)
    Do you have this in a different color?
  • 大きいサイズはありますか？ (Ookii saizu wa arimasu ka?)
    Do you have this in a larger size?
  • 袋をください。 (Fukuro wo kudasai.)
    Please give me a bag.
  • これだけです。 (Kore dake desu.)
    This is all/Just this one.

Tax-Free Shopping in Japan

As a traveler, you can benefit from tax-free shopping in Japan! Here's what you need to know:

Tax-Free Shopping Basics:

  • Eligibility: Available to foreign tourists staying in Japan for less than 6 months
  • Required documents: Your passport (must be shown at the time of purchase)
  • Minimum purchase: Usually 5,000 yen per store (may vary)
  • Categories:
    • General goods: Electronics, clothing, watches, etc.
    • Consumables: Food, drinks, cosmetics, medicines, etc.
  • Important: Consumable items must be kept sealed and taken out of Japan within 30 days

How to Ask for Tax-Free Shopping:

免税になりますか？ (Menzei ni narimasu ka?)
Can this be tax-free?

パスポートはこちらです。 (Pasupooto wa kochira desu.)
Here's my passport.

Cultural note: Look for stores with the "TAX-FREE" sign, particularly in tourist areas like Shinjuku, Shibuya, and Akihabara in Tokyo. Major department stores and electronics shops usually offer tax-free services.
