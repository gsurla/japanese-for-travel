Yesterday, we learned vocabulary and phrases related to izakayas. Let's review some key phrases:

  • おすすめは何ですか？ (Osusume wa nan desu ka?) - What do you recommend?
  • お会計をお願いします。 (O-kaikei o onegai shimasu.) - The bill, please.

Emergency Phrases & Medical Vocabulary

While we hope you never need to use these phrases during your trip to Japan, being prepared for emergencies is essential. Today, we'll learn vocabulary and phrases that can help you communicate your needs in case of an emergency or if you need medical assistance.

Cultural Note:

Japan has an excellent healthcare system, but language barriers can be challenging. In major cities, some hospitals have English-speaking staff or interpreters. Look for hospitals labeled as 国際診療部 (kokusai shinryō-bu) or "International Medical Care." The emergency number in Japan is 119 for both ambulance and fire services.

Emergency Phrases

助けて！ (たすけて！)

Tasukete!

Help!

緊急です！ (きんきゅうです！)

Kinkyū desu!

It's an emergency!

救急車を呼んでください。 (きゅうきゅうしゃをよんでください。)

Kyūkyūsha o yonde kudasai.

Please call an ambulance.

警察を呼んでください。 (けいさつをよんでください。)

Keisatsu o yonde kudasai.

Please call the police.

病院はどこですか？ (びょういんはどこですか？)

Byōin wa doko desu ka?

Where is the hospital?

薬局はどこですか？ (やっきょくはどこですか？)

Yakkyoku wa doko desu ka?

Where is the pharmacy?

Practice: At a Pharmacy

Example Dialogue:

You: すみません、薬を買いたいです。 (Sumimasen, kusuri o kaitai desu.)
Excuse me, I'd like to buy medicine.

Pharmacist: どのような症状ですか？ (Dono yōna shōjō desu ka?)
What are your symptoms?

You: 頭が痛いです。 (Atama ga itai desu.)
I have a headache.

Pharmacist: これをお勧めします。一日３回飲んでください。 (Kore o osusume shimasu. Ichinichi san-kai nonde kudasai.)
I recommend this. Please take it three times a day.

You: アレルギーがあります。大丈夫ですか？ (Arerugī ga arimasu. Daijōbu desu ka?)
I have allergies. Is this okay?

Pharmacist: どんなアレルギーですか？ (Donna arerugī desu ka?)
What kind of allergies do you have?

You: ペニシリンのアレルギーです。 (Penishirin no arerugī desu.)
I'm allergic to penicillin.

Pharmacist: この薬は大丈夫です。アレルギー物質は入っていません。 (Kono kusuri wa daijōbu desu. Arerugī busshitsu wa haitte imasen.)
This medicine is fine. It doesn't contain any allergens.

You: ありがとうございます。いくらですか？ (Arigatō gozaimasu. Ikura desu ka?)
Thank you very much. How much is it?

Pharmacist: 850円です。 (Happyaku gojū-en desu.)
It's 850 yen.

Homework

  1. Memorize the emergency phrases and important medical vocabulary.
  2. Practice the dialogue with a partner, switching roles between patient and pharmacist.
  3. Create a small medical card in Japanese describing any allergies or medical conditions you have.
  4. Find the locations of international hospitals or clinics in the areas you plan to visit in Japan.
  5. Practice pointing to different body parts while saying their names in Japanese.
