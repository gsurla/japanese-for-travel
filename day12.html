Restaurant Basics in Japan

Dining out in Japan is a delightful experience with unique customs. Let's learn the essentials for a smooth restaurant experience:

Restaurant Etiquette:

  • Entering: When you enter a restaurant, staff will typically greet you with "いらっしゃいませ" (irasshaimase - welcome). You don't need to respond, simply acknowledge with a nod or smile.
  • Seating: Wait to be seated unless there's a clear "seat yourself" policy.
  • Water: In most restaurants, water is served automatically for free.
  • Oshibori: You'll often receive a wet towel (おしぼり - oshibori) before your meal. Use it to clean your hands.
  • Chopsticks: Try to use chopsticks if possible. If you need a fork, it's fine to ask.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not customary in Japan. Service charge is usually included.
  • Calling staff: To call a server, say "すみません" (sumimasen - excuse me) or raise your hand.
  • Bill payment: In many restaurants, you pay at the register, not at the table.

Restaurant Vocabulary

Let's learn essential vocabulary for dining out in Japan:

Basic Restaurant Vocabulary:

レストラン (resutoran) / 食堂 (shokudō) - restaurant

Pronunciation: re-su-to-ran / sho-ku-dou

Example: 近くにレストランはありますか？ (Chikaku ni resutoran wa arimasu ka?) - Is there a restaurant nearby?

Cultural note: Japan has many types of restaurants specializing in specific dishes

メニュー (menyū) - menu

Pronunciation: me-nyuu

Example: メニューを見せてください (Menyū wo misete kudasai) - Please show me the menu

Cultural note: Many restaurants in Japan display plastic food models or picture menus

注文 (chūmon) - order

Pronunciation: chuu-mon

Example: 注文してもいいですか？ (Chūmon shite mo ii desu ka?) - May I order?

Cultural note: In some casual restaurants, you order by filling out a form

お勘定 (o-kanjō) / お会計 (o-kaikei) - bill/check

Pronunciation: o-kan-jou / o-kai-kei

Example: お勘定をお願いします (O-kanjō wo onegai shimasu) - The bill, please

Cultural note: In many restaurants, you take your bill to the cashier rather than paying at the table

Types of Restaurants:

  • 和食レストラン (washoku resutoran) - Japanese restaurant
  • 寿司屋 (sushi-ya) - sushi restaurant
  • ラーメン屋 (rāmen-ya) - ramen shop
  • 居酒屋 (izakaya) - Japanese pub/bar
  • 焼肉店 (yakiniku-ten) - Korean BBQ restaurant
  • ファミレス (famiresu) - family restaurant
  • カフェ (kafe) - café
  • ファストフード (fasuto fūdo) - fast food
  • 牛丼屋 (gyūdon-ya) - beef bowl restaurant

Food Categories:

  • 前菜 (zensai) - appetizer
  • メイン (mein) - main dish
  • デザート (dezāto) - dessert
  • 飲み物 (nomimono) - drinks
  • おすすめ (osusume) - recommendation/special
  • セット (setto) - set meal
  • 定食 (teishoku) - set meal with rice, soup, and sides
  • ランチ (ranchi) - lunch (often a special lunch menu)

Utensils and Tableware:

  • お箸 (o-hashi) - chopsticks
  • フォーク (fōku) - fork
  • ナイフ (naifu) - knife
  • スプーン (supūn) - spoon
  • お皿 (o-sara) - plate
  • グラス (gurasu) - glass
  • カップ (kappu) - cup
  • おしぼり (oshibori) - wet hand towel

Common Food Ingredients:

  • 肉 (niku) - meat
  • 牛肉 (gyūniku) - beef
  • 豚肉 (butaniku) - pork
  • 鶏肉 (toriniku) - chicken
  • 魚 (sakana) - fish
  • 野菜 (yasai) - vegetables
  • ご飯 (gohan) - rice
  • 麺 (men) - noodles

Essential Restaurant Phrases

Let's learn key phrases that will help you navigate restaurants in Japan:

1. ～人です (... nin desu) - Table for ... (people)

Pronunciation: ... nin des

Usage: Use this phrase when entering a restaurant to indicate how many people are in your party

Structure:

  • 数字 (number)
  • 人 (nin) - people counter
  • です (desu) - to be

Counter for people:

  • 1人 (hitori) - one person
  • 2人 (futari) - two people
  • 3人 (sannin) - three people
  • 4人 (yonin) - four people
  • 5人 (gonin) - five people

Example dialogue:

Staff: いらっしゃいませ！ (Irasshaimase!)
Welcome!

You: 2人です。 (Futari desu.)
Table for two, please.

Staff: こちらへどうぞ。 (Kochira e douzo.)
This way, please.

2. メニューをお願いします (Menyū wo onegai shimasu) - Menu, please

Pronunciation: me-nyuu wo o-ne-gai shi-mas

Usage: Use this phrase if a menu hasn't been provided yet

Structure:

  • メニュー (menyū) - menu
  • を (wo) - object marker
  • お願いします (onegai shimasu) - please

Example dialogue:

You: すみません、メニューをお願いします。 (Sumimasen, menyū wo onegai shimasu.)
Excuse me, menu please.

Server: はい、少々お待ちください。 (Hai, shōshō omachi kudasai.)
Yes, please wait a moment.

3. おすすめは何ですか？ (Osusume wa nan desu ka?) - What do you recommend?

Pronunciation: o-su-su-me wa nan des-ka

Usage: Use this phrase to ask for menu recommendations

Structure:

  • おすすめ (osusume) - recommendation
  • は (wa) - topic marker
  • 何ですか (nan desu ka) - what is?

Example dialogue:

You: おすすめは何ですか？ (Osusume wa nan desu ka?)
What do you recommend?

Server: 今日は牛丼がおすすめです。 (Kyō wa gyūdon ga osusume desu.)
Today, I recommend the beef bowl.

You: じゃあ、それをお願いします。 (Jaa, sore wo onegai shimasu.)
Then, I'll have that, please.

4. ～を注文したいです (... wo chūmon shitai desu) - I would like to order ...

Pronunciation: ... wo chuu-mon shi-tai des

Usage: Use this phrase to place your order

Structure:

  • ～ (dish name)
  • を (wo) - object marker
  • 注文したい (chūmon shitai) - want to order
  • です (desu) - to be

Example dialogue:

Server: ご注文は何にしますか？ (Go-chūmon wa nani ni shimasu ka?)
What would you like to order?

You: ラーメンを注文したいです。 (Rāmen wo chūmon shitai desu.)
I would like to order ramen.

Server: かしこまりました。 (Kashikomarimashita.)
Understood/I got it.

Simplified version:

You can also simply say the dish name followed by "お願いします" (onegai shimasu):

Example: ラーメンをお願いします。 (Rāmen wo onegai shimasu.) - Ramen, please.

5. お勘定をお願いします (O-kanjō wo onegai shimasu) - Bill, please

Pronunciation: o-kan-jou wo o-ne-gai shi-mas

Usage: Use this phrase when you're ready to pay

Structure:

  • お勘定 (o-kanjō) - bill
  • を (wo) - object marker
  • お願いします (onegai shimasu) - please

Example dialogue:

You: すみません、お勘定をお願いします。 (Sumimasen, o-kanjō wo onegai shimasu.)
Excuse me, bill please.

Server: はい、少々お待ちください。 (Hai, shōshō omachi kudasai.)
Yes, please wait a moment.

Variation:

You can also say:

お会計お願いします。 (O-kaikei onegai shimasu.) - Check, please.

Additional Useful Restaurant Phrases:

  • 予約はできますか？ (Yoyaku wa dekimasu ka?)
    Can I make a reservation?
  • 水をください。 (Mizu wo kudasai.)
    Water, please.
  • 英語のメニューはありますか？ (Eigo no menyū wa arimasu ka?)
    Do you have an English menu?
  • これは何ですか？ (Kore wa nan desu ka?)
    What is this?
  • アレルギーがあります。 (Arerugī ga arimasu.)
    I have an allergy.
  • 辛いですか？ (Karai desu ka?)
    Is it spicy?
  • 美味しいです！ (Oishii desu!)
    It's delicious!
  • ごちそうさまでした。 (Gochisōsama deshita.)
    Thank you for the meal. (Said when finishing eating)

Communicating Dietary Restrictions

If you have dietary restrictions, it's important to communicate them clearly. Here are some helpful phrases:

Allergies and Dietary Restrictions:

  • アレルギーがあります (Arerugī ga arimasu)
    I have an allergy
  • ～が食べられません (... ga taberaremasen)
    I cannot eat ...
  • ベジタリアンです (Bejitarian desu)
    I am vegetarian
  • ビーガンです (Bīgan desu)
    I am vegan
  • 肉なしでお願いします (Niku nashi de onegai shimasu)
    Without meat, please
  • グルテンフリーをお願いします (Guruten furī wo onegai shimasu)
    Gluten-free, please

Common Food Allergens in Japanese:

  • 海老/エビ (ebi) - shrimp
  • カニ (kani) - crab
  • 卵/たまご (tamago) - egg
  • 牛乳 (gyūnyū) - milk
  • 小麦 (komugi) - wheat
  • そば (soba) - buckwheat
  • ピーナッツ (pīnattsu) - peanuts
  • 魚 (sakana) - fish
  • 大豆 (daizu) - soy

Cultural note: Vegetarianism and veganism are less common in Japan, and there might be misunderstandings about what these terms mean exactly. It's helpful to be specific about what you can and cannot eat. Also, many Japanese dishes contain dashi (fish stock) that might not be immediately apparent.
