Comprehensive Review

Congratulations on reaching Day 19 of your Japanese language journey! Today, we'll consolidate everything you've learned so far through comprehensive review and practical application exercises. This session will help reinforce your knowledge and prepare you to use Japanese confidently during your travels.

Review Day 18: Emergency Phrases & Medical Vocabulary

Yesterday, we learned important emergency phrases and medical vocabulary. Let's review some key phrases:

  • 助けて！ (Tasukete!) - Help!
  • 具合が悪いです。 (Guai ga warui desu.) - I feel sick/unwell.
  • 病院はどこですか？ (Byōin wa doko desu ka?) - Where is the hospital?
  • 頭が痛いです。 (Atama ga itai desu.) - I have a headache.

Writing Systems Review

Let's review the Japanese writing systems we've learned:

Hiragana

Hiragana is used for native Japanese words and grammatical elements. You've learned all hiragana characters and can now read words written in this script.

Example: こんにちは (konnichiwa) - Hello

Katakana

Katakana is used primarily for foreign loanwords, foreign names, and emphasis. You've learned key katakana characters useful for recognizing words derived from English.

Example: レストラン (resutoran) - Restaurant

Kanji

We've introduced some essential kanji characters for travel. While you're not expected to write them, recognizing these characters will be helpful during your trip.

Examples: 入口 (iriguchi) - Entrance, 出口 (deguchi) - Exit

Grammar Structure Review

Basic Sentence Structure

The basic Japanese sentence follows a Subject-Object-Verb pattern:

私はすしを食べます。 (Watashi wa sushi o tabemasu.)

I eat sushi.

Particles

Particles are essential in Japanese grammar. Here are the key particles we've covered:

  • は (wa) - Topic marker
  • を (o) - Object marker
  • に (ni) - Direction/location marker
  • で (de) - Location where an action takes place
  • と (to) - "And" or "with"
  • か (ka) - Question marker

Verb Forms

We've learned verbs in the polite form (-masu form), which is appropriate for travel situations:

  • 行きます (ikimasu) - to go
  • 食べます (tabemasu) - to eat
  • 飲みます (nomimasu) - to drink
  • 見ます (mimasu) - to see/watch
  • 買います (kaimasu) - to buy

Practical Application Exercises

Scenario 1: Arriving at the Airport

Imagine you've just arrived at Narita Airport in Tokyo. Practice how you would:

  1. Ask where to find the train to Tokyo Station
  2. Buy a ticket from the ticket machine
  3. Ask a station attendant if this is the right platform
  4. Ask a fellow passenger if this train stops at Tokyo Station

You: すみません、東京駅への電車はどこですか？ (Sumimasen, Tōkyō-eki e no densha wa doko desu ka?)
Excuse me, where is the train to Tokyo Station?

Station Staff: B1階です。エスカレーターで下に行ってください。 (B1-kai desu. Esukarētā de shita ni itte kudasai.)
It's on floor B1. Please go down using the escalator.

You: ありがとうございます。 (Arigatō gozaimasu.)
Thank you very much.

[At the ticket machine]

You: すみません、東京駅までの切符を買いたいです。 (Sumimasen, Tōkyō-eki made no kippu o kaitai desu.)
Excuse me, I want to buy a ticket to Tokyo Station.

Scenario 2: Ordering at a Restaurant

You're at a restaurant and want to order food. Practice the conversation:

You: こんにちは。 (Konnichiwa.)
Hello.

Staff: いらっしゃいませ。何名様ですか？ (Irasshaimase. Nan-mei-sama desu ka?)
Welcome. How many people?

You: 二人です。 (Futari desu.)
Two people.

[After being seated]

You: おすすめは何ですか？ (Osusume wa nan desu ka?)
What do you recommend?

Staff: 今日は寿司セットがおすすめです。 (Kyō wa sushi setto ga osusume desu.)
Today's recommendation is the sushi set.

You: じゃあ、寿司セットをください。あと、水をお願いします。 (Jā, sushi setto o kudasai. Ato, mizu o onegai shimasu.)
Then, I'll have the sushi set. Also, water please.

Scenario 3: Shopping for Souvenirs

You're shopping for souvenirs and want to ask about prices and make purchases:

You: すみません、これはいくらですか？ (Sumimasen, kore wa ikura desu ka?)
Excuse me, how much is this?

Shop Staff: それは2,500円です。 (Sore wa 2,500-en desu.)
That's 2,500 yen.

You: これも買いたいです。合わせていくらですか？ (Kore mo kaitai desu. Awasete ikura desu ka?)
I want to buy this too. How much is it altogether?

Shop Staff: 合計で4,300円になります。 (Gōkei de 4,300-en ni narimasu.)
The total comes to 4,300 yen.

You: カードで払えますか？ (Kādo de haraemasu ka?)
Can I pay by card?

Homework

  1. Create a personalized travel phrasebook with the most useful phrases for your trip.
  2. Practice all the scenario dialogues out loud, recording yourself if possible.
  3. Write a short paragraph in Japanese about your travel plans, using vocabulary and grammar structures you've learned.
  4. Create flashcards for any vocabulary or phrases you find particularly challenging.
  5. Plan out specific language needs for your itinerary (e.g., phrases you'll need at specific attractions, restaurants, or accommodations).
  6. Practice reading signs and notices in Japanese using images from travel websites or guidebooks.
