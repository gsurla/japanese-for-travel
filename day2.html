Review Day 1 (5 minutes)

Let's quickly review the basic greetings we learned yesterday:

  • こんにちは (Konnichiwa) - Hello
  • おはようございます (Ohayou gozaimasu) - Good morning
  • こんばんは (Konbanwa) - Good evening
  • さようなら (Sayounara) - Goodbye
  • ありがとうございます (Arigatou gozaimasu) - Thank you

Also remember the self-introduction formula: わたしは [name] です (Watashi wa [name] desu) - I am [name]

Basic Hiragana (10 minutes)

Today we'll learn our first set of hiragana characters. These will form the foundation of your Japanese reading skills.

Vowels

Hiragana Romaji Pronunciation
あ a Like the "a" in "father"
い i Like the "ee" in "feet"
う u Like the "oo" in "boot" but with less rounded lips
え e Like the "e" in "pet"
お o Like the "o" in "go"

K-row

Hiragana Romaji Pronunciation
か ka Like "ka" in "karate"
き ki Like "key"
く ku Like "coo"
け ke Like "keh"
こ ko Like "ko" in "kona" (coffee)

Numbers 1-10 (10 minutes)

Learning Japanese numbers will help you with shopping, transportation, and more.

Number Kanji Hiragana Romaji Notes
1 一 いち ichi
2 二 に ni
3 三 さん san
4 四 よん / し yon / shi よん (yon) is more common as し (shi) sounds like death
5 五 ご go
6 六 ろく roku
7 七 なな / しち nana / shichi なな (nana) is more common
8 八 はち hachi
9 九 きゅう / く kyuu / ku きゅう (kyuu) is more common
10 十 じゅう juu

Practice Activities

Here are some activities to practice your new skills:

  • Practice writing each hiragana character 5 times
  • Practice counting from 1 to 10 in Japanese
  • Try to identify these characters when you see them

Check Your Understanding: Day 2 Quiz

Test your knowledge with this quick quiz on what you've learned today.

1. What is the hiragana character for "i"?

2. What is the Japanese number for "7"?

3. What is the romaji for the hiragana character "く"?

4. Which number is よん (yon) in Japanese?

5. What is the hiragana for "o"?

Homework/Practice Activities

  1. Practice writing all the hiragana characters covered today (あ、い、う、え、お、か、き、く、け、こ)
  2. Practice counting from 1 to 10 out loud in Japanese
  3. Create flashcards for the hiragana you learned today
  4. Try to find these characters in Japanese products or media
  5. Combine hiragana to write simple words like "かお" (kao - face) or "あか" (aka - red)
