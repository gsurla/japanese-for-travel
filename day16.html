Introduction to Popular Japanese Destinations

Today, we'll explore vocabulary and phrases related to popular destinations and cultural activities in Japan. This will help you navigate tourist spots, participate in cultural experiences, and communicate your interests during your travels.

Review from Day 15

Yesterday, we covered telephoning and making reservations in Japanese. Let's review a few key phrases:

  • 予約をしたいです。(Yoyaku o shitai desu.) - I would like to make a reservation.
  • ～時に予約できますか？(～ji ni yoyaku dekimasu ka?) - Can I make a reservation for ～ o'clock?
  • 電話番号は何番ですか？(Denwa bangō wa nanban desu ka?) - What is the phone number?

Useful Phrases for Tourists

These phrases will help you navigate tourist attractions and participate in cultural activities:

～はどこにありますか？

～ wa doko ni arimasu ka?

Where is ～?

Example: 博物館はどこにありますか？(Hakubutsukan wa doko ni arimasu ka?) - Where is the museum?

～までどのくらいかかりますか？

～ made dono kurai kakarimasu ka?

How long does it take to get to ～?

Example: 東京駅までどのくらいかかりますか？(Tōkyō-eki made dono kurai kakarimasu ka?) - How long does it take to get to Tokyo Station?

～に行きたいです。

～ ni ikitai desu.

I want to go to ～.

Example: 京都に行きたいです。(Kyōto ni ikitai desu.) - I want to go to Kyoto.

～を体験したいです。

～ o taiken shitai desu.

I want to experience ～.

Example: 茶道を体験したいです。(Sadō o taiken shitai desu.) - I want to experience tea ceremony.

入場料はいくらですか？

Nyūjōryō wa ikura desu ka?

How much is the admission fee?

写真を撮ってもいいですか？

Shashin o totte mo ii desu ka?

May I take photos?

おすすめの場所はどこですか？

Osusume no basho wa doko desu ka?

Where are the recommended places?

この近くに～はありますか？

Kono chikaku ni ～ wa arimasu ka?

Is there a ～ nearby?

Example: この近くに温泉はありますか？(Kono chikaku ni onsen wa arimasu ka?) - Is there a hot spring nearby?

Cultural Notes

Temple and Shrine Etiquette

When visiting Japanese temples (お寺, o-tera) and shrines (神社, jinja), there are some important customs to observe:

  • At the entrance of shrines, you'll find a water basin (手水舎, temizuya) for ritual purification. Use the ladle to wash your left hand, then your right hand, then rinse your mouth (don't drink the water), and finally wash the handle of the ladle.
  • At temples, you may need to remove your shoes before entering certain buildings. Look for signs or shoe racks.
  • Bow slightly when entering sacred areas.
  • At shrines, it's customary to bow twice, clap twice, make a wish, and bow once more when praying.
  • Speak quietly and be respectful of the sacred space.

Onsen (Hot Spring) Etiquette

Visiting an onsen (温泉) is a quintessential Japanese experience, but there are specific rules to follow:

  • You must wash thoroughly at the washing stations before entering the bath.
  • No swimwear is allowed – onsen bathing is done nude.
  • Take a small towel with you to maintain some modesty while walking around, but don't put it in the water.
  • If you have tattoos, check in advance if they're allowed, as some onsen prohibit them.
  • Don't swim, splash, or be noisy in the bath.

Practice Dialogue: Visiting a Tourist Attraction

あなた (You): すみません、金閣寺はどこにありますか？
Sumimasen, Kinkaku-ji wa doko ni arimasu ka?
Excuse me, where is Kinkaku-ji?

地元の人 (Local person): この道をまっすぐ行って、右に曲がってください。バス停もあります。
Kono michi o massugu itte, migi ni magatte kudasai. Basu tei mo arimasu.
Go straight down this road and turn right. There's also a bus stop.

あなた (You): ありがとうございます。金閣寺まで歩いてどのくらいかかりますか？
Arigatō gozaimasu. Kinkaku-ji made aruite dono kurai kakarimasu ka?
Thank you. How long does it take to walk to Kinkaku-ji?

地元の人 (Local person): 歩いて約20分です。バスなら10分くらいです。
Aruite yaku nijuppun desu. Basu nara juppun kurai desu.
It's about 20 minutes on foot. By bus, it's around 10 minutes.

あなた (You): なるほど。入場料はいくらですか？
Naruhodo. Nyūjōryō wa ikura desu ka?
I see. How much is the admission fee?

地元の人 (Local person): 大人は400円です。
Otona wa yonhyaku-en desu.
It's 400 yen for adults.

あなた (You): 写真を撮ってもいいですか？
Shashin o totte mo ii desu ka?
May I take photos?

地元の人 (Local person): はい、外では大丈夫です。でも、建物の中ではだめです。
Hai, soto dewa daijōbu desu. Demo, tatemono no naka dewa dame desu.
Yes, it's fine outside. But it's not allowed inside the building.

あなた (You): わかりました。どうもありがとうございました。
Wakarimashita. Dōmo arigatō gozaimashita.
I understand. Thank you very much.
