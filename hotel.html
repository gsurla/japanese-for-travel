Checking into a Hotel

Navigating hotel check-in and services in Japan can be much easier with these essential phrases. From arrival to check-out, this guide covers everything you need for a smooth hotel experience.

Arrival and Check-in

Japanese Pronunciation English
予約があります。 Yoyaku ga arimasu. I have a reservation.
名前は＿＿です。 Namae wa __ desu. My name is __.
パスポートを見せてください。 Pasupooto wo misete kudasai. Please show me your passport.
こちらにサインをお願いします。 Kochira ni sain wo onegaishimasu. Please sign here.
クレジットカードをお願いします。 Kurejitto kaado wo onegaishimasu. Credit card, please.

Room Information

Japanese Pronunciation English
部屋番号は何番ですか？ Heya bangou wa nanban desu ka? What is my room number?
鍵をください。 Kagi wo kudasai. Please give me the key.
エレベーターはどこですか？ Erebeetaa wa doko desu ka? Where is the elevator?
荷物を部屋まで運んでいただけますか？ Nimotsu wo heya made hakonde itadakemasu ka? Could you bring my luggage to the room?
Wi-Fiのパスワードは何ですか？ Wi-Fi no pasuwaado wa nan desu ka? What is the Wi-Fi password?

Hotel Amenities

Japanese Pronunciation English
朝食は何時からですか？ Choushoku wa nanji kara desu ka? What time does breakfast start?
朝食はどこですか？ Choushoku wa doko desu ka? Where is breakfast served?
プールはありますか？ Puuru wa arimasu ka? Is there a pool?
大浴場はありますか？ Daiyokujou wa arimasu ka? Is there a public bath?
ジムはありますか？ Jimu wa arimasu ka? Is there a gym?

During Your Stay

Japanese Pronunciation English
部屋を掃除してください。 Heya wo souji shite kudasai. Please clean my room.
今日は掃除しないでください。 Kyou wa souji shinaide kudasai. Please don't clean today.
タオルをもっとください。 Taoru wo motto kudasai. More towels, please.
枕をもう一つください。 Makura wo mou hitotsu kudasai. One more pillow, please.
エアコンが動きません。 Eakon ga ugoikimasen. The air conditioner isn't working.

Check-out

Japanese Pronunciation English
チェックアウトをお願いします。 Chekkuauto wo onegaishimasu. I'd like to check out.
チェックアウト時間は何時ですか？ Chekkuauto jikan wa nanji desu ka? What time is check-out?
遅めのチェックアウトは可能ですか？ Osomeno chekkuauto wa kanou desu ka? Is a late check-out possible?
荷物を預かっていただけますか？ Nimotsu wo azukatte itadakemasu ka? Could you store my luggage?
タクシーを呼んでいただけますか？ Takushii wo yonde itadakemasu ka? Could you call a taxi for me?

Japanese Hotel Tips

  • Types of Accommodations: Japan offers various types of accommodations including Western-style hotels, traditional ryokans (Japanese inns), business hotels, and capsule hotels.
  • Slippers: Many hotels provide slippers for use inside the room. Remove your shoes at the entrance.
  • Bathroom Amenities: Japanese hotels typically provide extensive toiletries including toothbrushes, razors, combs, and other personal items.
  • Room Size: Hotel rooms in Japan, especially in major cities, tend to be smaller than Western standards.
  • Onsen Hotels: Some hotels have onsen (hot spring baths). These are communal and typically gender-separated. Tattoos may be prohibited in some onsen.
  • Payment: While credit cards are widely accepted at hotels, it's good to have some cash on hand for smaller establishments.
