Basic Weather Vocabulary

Weather can significantly impact your travel plans, so understanding weather-related vocabulary is important. Let's learn key terms for discussing weather in Japanese:

天気 (tenki) - weather

Pronunciation: ten-ki

Example: 今日の天気はどうですか？ (Kyou no tenki wa dou desu ka?) - How is the weather today?

Cultural note: Weather forecasts are taken very seriously in Japan, and many people check them daily

晴れ (hare) - sunny/clear

Pronunciation: ha-re

Example: 明日は晴れです (Ashita wa hare desu) - Tomorrow will be sunny

Cultural note: Many Japanese festivals and events are planned around good weather days

雨 (ame) - rain

Pronunciation: a-me

Example: 雨が降っています (Ame ga futte imasu) - It's raining

Cultural note: Japan has a rainy season (梅雨 - tsuyu) from June to July in most regions

曇り (kumori) - cloudy

Pronunciation: ku-mo-ri

Example: 今日は曇りです (Kyou wa kumori desu) - Today is cloudy

Cultural note: Partially cloudy days are common in spring and fall

雪 (yuki) - snow

Pronunciation: yu-ki

Example: 雪が降っています (Yuki ga futte imasu) - It's snowing

Cultural note: Northern Japan, especially Hokkaido, gets significant snowfall in winter

暑い (atsui) - hot (weather)

Pronunciation: a-tsu-i

Example: 今日はとても暑いです (Kyou wa totemo atsui desu) - Today is very hot

Cultural note: Japanese summers can be extremely hot and humid, especially in August

寒い (samui) - cold (weather)

Pronunciation: sa-mu-i

Example: 冬は寒いです (Fuyu wa samui desu) - Winter is cold

Cultural note: Central heating is not common in many Japanese homes, making winters feel colder indoors

Additional Weather Vocabulary:

  • 台風 (taifuu) - typhoon
  • 霧 (kiri) - fog
  • 風 (kaze) - wind
  • 強い風 (tsuyoi kaze) - strong wind
  • 雷 (kaminari) - thunder
  • 稲妻 (inazuma) - lightning
  • 湿度 (shitsudo) - humidity
  • 気温 (kion) - temperature
  • 予報 (yohou) - forecast

Seasons:

  • 春 (haru) - spring
  • 夏 (natsu) - summer
  • 秋 (aki) - autumn/fall
  • 冬 (fuyu) - winter
  • 梅雨 (tsuyu) - rainy season (early summer)

Weather-Related Verbs:

  • 降る (furu) - to fall (rain, snow)
  • 晴れる (hareru) - to clear up
  • 曇る (kumoru) - to become cloudy
  • 変わる (kawaru) - to change

Weather Phrases

Let's learn essential phrases for discussing weather in Japanese:

今日の天気はどうですか？
Kyou no tenki wa dou desu ka?
How is the weather today?
Use this phrase to ask about the current weather
  • 今日 (kyou) - today
  • の (no) - possessive particle
  • 天気 (tenki) - weather
  • は (wa) - topic marker
  • どう (dou) - how
  • ですか (desu ka) - is?

Variations:

  • 明日の天気はどうですか？ (Ashita no tenki wa dou desu ka?) - How will the weather be tomorrow?
  • 週末の天気はどうですか？ (Shuumatsu no tenki wa dou desu ka?) - How will the weather be this weekend?
雨が降っています。
Ame ga futte imasu.
It's raining.
Use this phrase to describe current rain
  • 雨 (ame) - rain
  • が (ga) - subject marker
  • 降って (futte) - falling
  • います (imasu) - is (continuous state)

Variations:

  • 雪が降っています (Yuki ga futte imasu) - It's snowing
  • 大雨です (Ooame desu) - It's raining heavily
  • 小雨です (Kosame desu) - It's drizzling
暑いですね。
Atsui desu ne.
It's hot, isn't it?
Use this phrase to comment on hot weather
  • 暑い (atsui) - hot
  • です (desu) - is
  • ね (ne) - isn't it? (seeking agreement)

Variations:

  • 蒸し暑いですね (Mushiatsui desu ne) - It's humid and hot, isn't it?
  • 暑すぎます (Atsusugimasu) - It's too hot
寒いですね。
Samui desu ne.
It's cold, isn't it?
Use this phrase to comment on cold weather
  • 寒い (samui) - cold
  • です (desu) - is
  • ね (ne) - isn't it? (seeking agreement)

Variations:

  • 寒すぎます (Samusugimasu) - It's too cold
  • 肌寒いです (Hadazamui desu) - It's chilly
天気予報によると、明日は晴れです。
Tenki yohou ni yoru to, ashita wa hare desu.
According to the weather forecast, tomorrow will be sunny.
Use this phrase to discuss weather forecasts
  • 天気予報 (tenki yohou) - weather forecast
  • によると (ni yoru to) - according to
  • 明日 (ashita) - tomorrow
  • は (wa) - topic marker
  • 晴れ (hare) - sunny
  • です (desu) - is

Variations:

  • 天気予報によると、明日は雨です (Tenki yohou ni yoru to, ashita wa ame desu) - According to the weather forecast, tomorrow will be rainy
  • 天気予報によると、週末は曇りです (Tenki yohou ni yoru to, shuumatsu wa kumori desu) - According to the weather forecast, the weekend will be cloudy

Seasonal Activities and Events

Japan has distinct seasons, each with its own activities and events. Let's learn vocabulary related to seasonal activities:

Spring (春 - haru):

  • 桜 (sakura) - cherry blossoms
  • 花見 (hanami) - cherry blossom viewing
  • 春分の日 (shunbun no hi) - Spring Equinox Day (national holiday)
  • 新学期 (shingakki) - new school term

A: 桜はもう咲いていますか？ (Sakura wa mou saite imasu ka?)
Are the cherry blossoms blooming yet?

B: はい、公園で花見ができますよ。 (Hai, kouen de hanami ga dekimasu yo.)
Yes, you can do hanami in the park.

Summer (夏 - natsu):

  • 海水浴 (kaisuiyoku) - sea bathing/going to the beach
  • 花火大会 (hanabi taikai) - fireworks festival
  • 祭り (matsuri) - festival
  • お盆 (obon) - Obon festival (Buddhist custom to honor ancestors)

A: 今週末、花火大会がありますか？ (Konshuumatsu, hanabi taikai ga arimasu ka?)
Is there a fireworks festival this weekend?

B: はい、川の近くであります。 (Hai, kawa no chikaku de arimasu.)
Yes, it's near the river.

Autumn (秋 - aki):

  • 紅葉 (kouyou) - autumn leaves
  • 秋分の日 (shuubun no hi) - Autumn Equinox Day (national holiday)
  • 収穫 (shuukaku) - harvest
  • 運動会 (undoukai) - sports day

A: 紅葉はどこで見られますか？ (Kouyou wa doko de miraremasku ka?)
Where can I see the autumn leaves?

B: 山に行くといいですよ。 (Yama ni iku to ii desu yo.)
It's good to go to the mountains.

Winter (冬 - fuyu):

  • スキー (sukii) - skiing
  • 雪祭り (yuki matsuri) - snow festival
  • こたつ (kotatsu) - heated table with blanket
  • 年末年始 (nenmatsu nenshi) - year-end and New Year period

A: 冬は日本で何をしますか？ (Fuyu wa Nihon de nani wo shimasu ka?)
What do you do in Japan during winter?

B: スキーに行きます。北海道が有名です。 (Sukii ni ikimasu. Hokkaido ga yuumei desu.)
I go skiing. Hokkaido is famous for it.
