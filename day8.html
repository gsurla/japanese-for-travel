Transportation Vocabulary (10 minutes)

Transportation vocabulary is essential for travelers in Japan. Let's learn key terms for getting around:

Basic Transportation Words:

バス (basu) - bus

Pronunciation: bah-soo

Example: バス停 (basu tei) - bus stop

Cultural note: Buses in Japan are very punctual. You typically board from the middle/rear door and exit from the front.

電車 (densha) - train

Pronunciation: den-shah

Example: 電車に乗ります (densha ni norimasu) - I ride the train

Cultural note: Japan's train system is renowned for its punctuality and efficiency.

タクシー (takushii) - taxi

Pronunciation: tah-koo-shee

Example: タクシーを呼びます (takushii wo yobimasu) - I call a taxi

Cultural note: Taxi doors in Japan open and close automatically - don't try to open or close them yourself!

地下鉄 (chikatetsu) - subway

Pronunciation: chee-kah-teh-tsoo

Example: 地下鉄の駅 (chikatetsu no eki) - subway station

Cultural note: Major cities like Tokyo, Osaka, and Kyoto have extensive subway systems.

切符 (kippu) - ticket

Pronunciation: kip-poo

Example: 切符を買います (kippu wo kaimasu) - I buy a ticket

Cultural note: Many stations have ticket machines with English options.

Additional Transportation Vocabulary:

  • 新幹線 (shinkansen) - bullet train
  • 駅 (eki) - station
  • 空港 (kuukou) - airport
  • 出口 (deguchi) - exit
  • 入口 (iriguchi) - entrance
  • 乗り場 (noriba) - boarding area/platform
  • 改札 (kaisatsu) - ticket gate
  • 乗り換え (norikae) - transfer (between trains/lines)
  • 遅延 (chien) - delay
  • 時刻表 (jikokuhyou) - timetable
  • 往復 (oufuku) - round trip
  • 片道 (katamichi) - one way

Transportation Verbs:

  • 乗ります (norimasu) - to ride/board
  • 降ります (orimasu) - to get off
  • 到着します (touchaku shimasu) - to arrive
  • 出発します (shuppatsu shimasu) - to depart
  • 歩きます (arukimasu) - to walk
  • 運転します (unten shimasu) - to drive

Useful Transportation Phrases (15 minutes)

Let's learn some essential phrases for navigating Japan's transportation system:

1. ＿＿行きのバスはどこですか？ (__ iki no basu wa doko desu ka?) - Where is the bus to __?

Pronunciation: __ ee-kee no bah-soo wah doh-koh des-kah?

Usage: Use this phrase when looking for a specific bus route

Structure:

  • [Destination] + 行き (iki) - bound for [destination]
  • の (no) - connecting particle
  • バス (basu) - bus
  • はどこですか (wa doko desu ka) - where is?

Example dialogue:

A: 東京駅行きのバスはどこですか？ (Tokyo eki iki no basu wa doko desu ka?)
Where is the bus to Tokyo Station?

B: バス停は向こうです。 (Basu tei wa mukou desu.)
The bus stop is over there.

Variations:

- 京都行きの電車はどこですか？ (Kyoto iki no densha wa doko desu ka?)
Where is the train to Kyoto?

- 空港行きのバスはどこですか？ (Kuukou iki no basu wa doko desu ka?)
Where is the bus to the airport?

2. ＿＿までいくらですか？ (__ made ikura desu ka?) - How much is it to __?

Pronunciation: __ mah-deh ee-koo-rah des-kah?

Usage: Use this phrase to ask about transportation fares

Structure:

  • [Destination] + まで (made) - to [destination]
  • いくらですか (ikura desu ka) - how much is it?

Example dialogue:

A: 新宿までいくらですか？ (Shinjuku made ikura desu ka?)
How much is it to Shinjuku?

B: 220円です。 (Nihyaku-nijuu-en desu.)
It's 220 yen.

Variations:

- タクシーで駅までいくらですか？ (Takushii de eki made ikura desu ka?)
How much is it to the station by taxi?

- 大阪まで新幹線でいくらですか？ (Osaka made shinkansen de ikura desu ka?)
How much is it to Osaka by bullet train?

3. 一枚お願いします (Ichimai onegaishimasu) - One ticket, please.

Pronunciation: ee-chee-my oh-neh-gah-ee-shee-mahs

Usage: Use this phrase when buying a single ticket

Structure:

  • 一枚 (ichimai) - one flat object (counter for tickets, papers, etc.)
  • お願いします (onegaishimasu) - please

Example dialogue:

A: 京都まで一枚お願いします。 (Kyoto made ichimai onegaishimasu.)
One ticket to Kyoto, please.

B: 1,420円です。 (Sen-yonhyaku-nijuu-en desu.)
It's 1,420 yen.

Variations:

- 二枚お願いします。 (Nimai onegaishimasu.)
Two tickets, please.

- 往復で一枚お願いします。 (Oufuku de ichimai onegaishimasu.)
One round-trip ticket, please.

Additional Useful Transportation Phrases:

  • 次の駅は何ですか？ (Tsugi no eki wa nan desu ka?)
    What is the next station?
  • この電車は＿＿に行きますか？ (Kono densha wa __ ni ikimasu ka?)
    Does this train go to __?
  • 何番線ですか？ (Nan-ban-sen desu ka?)
    Which platform/track number?
  • 何時に出発しますか？ (Nanji ni shuppatsu shimasu ka?)
    What time does it depart?
  • 遅れていますか？ (Okurete imasu ka?)
    Is it delayed?
  • 乗り換えはどこですか？ (Norikae wa doko desu ka?)
    Where is the transfer?
  • すみません、通ります。 (Sumimasen, toorimasu.)
    Excuse me, I'm passing through. (Useful in crowded trains)

IC Cards and Ticket Machines (5 minutes)

IC Cards (Transportation Cards)

In Japan, IC cards are rechargeable smart cards used for public transportation and shopping:

  1. Suica (スイカ) - Issued by JR East (Tokyo area)
  2. PASMO (パスモ) - Issued by Tokyo Metro and private railways
  3. ICOCA (イコカ) - Issued by JR West (Osaka/Kyoto area)

Benefits of IC Cards:

  • No need to buy individual tickets
  • Slight discount on fares
  • Can be used on most trains, buses, and subways nationwide
  • Can be used for purchases at convenience stores and vending machines
  • Refundable deposit (minus fee) when returning the card

How to Use an IC Card:

  • Touch the card to the card reader at the ticket gate when entering
  • Touch again when exiting (fare is automatically calculated)
  • Recharge at ticket machines when balance is low

Useful Phrases:

- チャージお願いします。 (Chaaji onegaishimasu.)
Please charge (my card).

- 千円チャージしてください。 (Sen-en chaaji shite kudasai.)
Please charge 1,000 yen.

Using Ticket Machines

Most ticket machines in Japan have English options. Here's how to use them:

  1. Look for the "English" button (usually in the top corner)
  2. Select your destination on the map or from a list
  3. Select ticket type (single, round-trip, etc.)
  4. Insert money (machines accept coins and bills)
  5. Take your ticket and change

Useful Vocabulary for Ticket Machines:

  • 行先 (ikisaki) - destination
  • 大人 (otona) - adult
  • 子供 (kodomo) - child
  • 片道 (katamichi) - one way
  • 往復 (oufuku) - round trip
  • 特急券 (tokkyuuken) - express ticket

Exercise 2: Transportation Dialogues

Practice these common transportation scenarios:

Scenario 1: Buying a Train Ticket

A: 東京駅まで一枚お願いします。
(Tokyo eki made ichimai onegaishimasu.)
One ticket to Tokyo Station, please.

B: 片道ですか、往復ですか？
(Katamichi desu ka, oufuku desu ka?)
One-way or round-trip?

A: 片道でお願いします。
(Katamichi de onegaishimasu.)
One-way, please.

B: 320円です。
(Sanbyaku-nijuu-en desu.)
It's 320 yen.

Scenario 2: Taking a Taxi

A: すみません、ホテルグランドパレスまでお願いします。
(Sumimasen, Hoteru Gurando Paresu made onegaishimasu.)
Excuse me, to Hotel Grand Palace, please.

B: わかりました。
(Wakarimashita.)
Understood.

A: だいたいいくらですか？
(Daitai ikura desu ka?)
Approximately how much will it be?

B: だいたい1,500円くらいです。
(Daitai sen-gohyaku-en kurai desu.)
Approximately 1,500 yen.
