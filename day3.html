Basic Question Phrases (10 minutes)

Now that we're building our hiragana knowledge, let's learn some basic question phrases that will be very useful during your travels.

これは何ですか？ (Kore wa nan desu ka?)

Pronunciation: koh-reh wah nahn dehs kah?

Meaning: What is this?

Use this when you want to know what something is.

Structure:

  • これ (kore) - this
  • は (wa) - topic marker
  • 何 (nan) - what
  • ですか (desu ka) - is? (question form)

どこですか？ (Doko desu ka?)

Pronunciation: doh-koh dehs kah?

Meaning: Where is it?

Use this to ask for locations.

Structure:

  • どこ (doko) - where
  • ですか (desu ka) - is? (question form)

いくらですか？ (Ikura desu ka?)

Pronunciation: ee-koo-rah dehs kah?

Meaning: How much is it?

Use this when shopping or when you want to know the price of something.

Structure:

  • いくら (ikura) - how much
  • ですか (desu ka) - is? (question form)

Forming Questions and Answers

In Japanese, questions are formed by adding か (ka) to the end of a statement. The word order typically remains the same as a statement.

Simple Answers:

  • はい (Hai) - Yes
  • いいえ (Iie) - No
  • わかりません (Wakarimasen) - I don't understand/I don't know
