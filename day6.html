Review Day 5 (5 minutes)

Let's quickly review the sentence structure and polite expressions we learned yesterday:

Basic Sentence Structure:

[Topic] は [Comment] です (Topic wa Comment desu) - "Topic is Comment"

Example: これはホテルです (Kore wa hoteru desu) - This is a hotel

Hiragana:

M-row: ま (ma), み (mi), む (mu), め (me), も (mo)

Y-row: や (ya), ゆ (yu), よ (yo)

R-row: ら (ra), り (ri)

Polite Expressions:

すみません (Sumimasen) - Excuse me/I'm sorry

お願いします (Onegaishimasu) - Please

失礼します (Shitsurei shimasu) - Excuse me (when interrupting/leaving)

Remaining Hiragana (10 minutes)

Today we'll complete our hiragana learning with the final characters and some basic combinations.

Rest of R-row

Hiragana Romaji Pronunciation
る ru Between "r" and "l", like in "rule"
れ re Between "r" and "l", like in "red"
ろ ro Between "r" and "l", like in "row"

W-row and N

Hiragana Romaji Pronunciation
わ wa Like "wa" in "water"
を wo Like "o" (often pronounced the same as お)
ん n Like "n" in "no" (standalone consonant)

Basic Combinations

When small versions of や (ya), ゆ (yu), and よ (yo) are combined with certain consonants, they form new sounds:

Hiragana Romaji Pronunciation
きゃ kya Like "kya" (k + ya)
しゃ sha Like "sha" in "shark"
ちゃ cha Like "cha" in "chart"
にゃ nya Like "nya" (n + ya)
みゃ mya Like "mya" (m + ya)

This same pattern works for all consonants with small ゆ (yu) and よ (yo): きゅ (kyu), しゅ (shu), ちゅ (chu), etc.

Essential Verbs (15 minutes)

Now that we've completed our hiragana learning, let's focus on some essential verbs that will be very useful during your travels.

Japanese Verb Basics

In Japanese, verbs always come at the end of the sentence and don't change form based on the subject (I, you, he, she, etc.).

The verbs we'll learn today are in the polite form ending in -ます (-masu), which is appropriate for most travel situations.

Essential Travel Verbs

Japanese Romaji English
行きます ikimasu to go
食べます tabemasu to eat
飲みます nomimasu to drink
見ます mimasu to see/watch
買います kaimasu to buy

Basic Verb Patterns

Now let's learn how to use these verbs in simple sentences:

1. [Place] に 行きます (Place ni ikimasu) - I go to [Place]

東京に行きます。

Tokyo ni ikimasu.

I go to Tokyo.

2. [Food] を 食べます (Food wo tabemasu) - I eat [Food]

寿司を食べます。

Sushi wo tabemasu.

I eat sushi.

3. [Drink] を 飲みます (Drink wo nomimasu) - I drink [Drink]

お茶を飲みます。

Ocha wo nomimasu.

I drink tea.

4. [Thing] を 見ます (Thing wo mimasu) - I see/watch [Thing]

富士山を見ます。

Fujisan wo mimasu.

I see Mt. Fuji.

5. [Item] を 買います (Item wo kaimasu) - I buy [Item]

お土産を買います。

Omiyage wo kaimasu.

I buy souvenirs.

Negative Forms

To make these verbs negative (I don't go, I don't eat, etc.), change the ending from -ます (-masu) to -ません (-masen):

Affirmative Negative English
行きます (ikimasu) 行きません (ikimasen) I go / I don't go
食べます (tabemasu) 食べません (tabemasen) I eat / I don't eat
飲みます (nomimasu) 飲みません (nomimasen) I drink / I don't drink
見ます (mimasu) 見ません (mimasen) I see / I don't see
買います (kaimasu) 買いません (kaimasen) I buy / I don't buy
