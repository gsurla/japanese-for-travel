Small Talk

Making small talk in Japanese can help you connect with locals and enhance your travel experience. These phrases will help you introduce yourself, have basic conversations, and make a positive impression.

Greetings and Introductions

Japanese Pronunciation English
はじめまして。 Hajimemashite. Nice to meet you.
わたしの名前は＿＿です。 Watashi no namae wa __ desu. My name is __.
＿＿から来ました。 __ kara kimashita. I'm from __.
日本は初めてです。 Nihon wa hajimete desu. This is my first time in Japan.
日本語が少し話せます。 Nihongo ga sukoshi hanasemasu. I can speak a little Japanese.

Basic Conversation

Japanese Pronunciation English
お元気ですか？ Ogenki desu ka? How are you?
元気です。 Genki desu. I'm fine.
お仕事は何ですか？ Oshigoto wa nan desu ka? What is your job?
趣味は何ですか？ Shumi wa nan desu ka? What are your hobbies?
家族はいますか？ Kazoku wa imasu ka? Do you have family?

Travel Talk

Japanese Pronunciation English
日本のどこに行きましたか？ Nihon no doko ni ikimashita ka? Where in Japan have you been?
＿＿が好きです。 __ ga suki desu. I like __.
日本料理は美味しいですね。 Nihon ryouri wa oishii desu ne. Japanese food is delicious, isn't it?
明日は＿＿に行きます。 Ashita wa __ ni ikimasu. Tomorrow I'm going to __.
日本は素晴らしいですね。 Nihon wa subarashii desu ne. Japan is wonderful, isn't it?

Weather Talk

Japanese Pronunciation English
今日はいい天気ですね。 Kyou wa ii tenki desu ne. Nice weather today, isn't it?
暑いですね。 Atsui desu ne. It's hot, isn't it?
寒いですね。 Samui desu ne. It's cold, isn't it?
雨が降っていますね。 Ame ga futte imasu ne. It's raining, isn't it?
明日の天気はどうですか？ Ashita no tenki wa dou desu ka? How will the weather be tomorrow?

Ending Conversations

Japanese Pronunciation English
お話しできて楽しかったです。 Ohanashi dekite tanoshikatta desu. It was nice talking with you.
また会いましょう。 Mata aimashou. Let's meet again.
さようなら。 Sayounara. Goodbye.
気をつけて。 Ki wo tsukete. Take care.
良い旅を。 Yoi tabi wo. Have a good trip.

Small Talk Tips in Japan

  • Bowing: A slight bow is common when greeting someone. The deeper the bow, the more respect is shown.
  • Business Cards: If exchanging business cards (meishi), offer and receive with both hands and a slight bow.
  • Personal Questions: Questions about age, marital status, and income that might seem intrusive in Western cultures are more common in Japanese small talk.
  • Compliments: Japanese people often respond to compliments by denying them out of modesty.
  • Silence: Comfortable silence is more acceptable in Japanese conversation than in many Western cultures.
  • Ending with "ne": Adding "ne" (ね) at the end of a sentence is like saying "isn't it?" and invites agreement, making conversation more friendly.
